Tera Capital Fund was launched in April 2005 as a fund of funds focused primarily on Russia. It was the first and remains the only fund of funds in the world with its specific geographic focus. It is also singular in that Altima Asset Management, the investment management company that manages Tera, charges no fees other than a success fee and also covers all of Tera's expenses.

Prior to the Fall 2008 liquidity crisis, Altima has guided Tera to net returns to investors exceeding 35% annually since inception. Even including the crisis (through February 2009) Tera had still provided its early investors with a positive rate of return. Tera has proven to be a particularly strong defensive investment. While Russian equity markets have fallen back to levels last reached almost 6 years ago and US equity markets have fallen back to levels reach more than 12 years ago, Tera has given up approximately 3.5 years of gains -- and is still above the level it reached in June 2005.

Among the more than 2,600 funds of funds worldwide ranked by Eurekahedge and out of the 46 months that Tera has been ranked by that company since inception, Tera has been ranked No. 1 in monthly performance three times, in the top 10 seven times, was ranked the best-performing fund of funds in the world for all of 2005, has been ranked No. 1 in performance YTD six times, has been ranked in the top 10 thirteen times, has been ranked No. 1 in terms of annualized performance ten times (most recently in September 2008), and has been ranked in the top 10 forty times.

During every general market downturn of more than 10% in Russia since its inception, Tera has fallen by a small fraction of the total market and of other funds: generally experiencing only approximately 30% of the overall percentage decrease. This has given it a Sharpe Ratio that is far higher than that of all major Russian indices and the average of other funds. The distinction has grown during the crisis, when Tera's Sharpe Ratio has remained significantly positive, while the Sharpe ratios of all Russian indices and almost every Russian fund have turned negative.
